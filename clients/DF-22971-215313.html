Hannah, today you'll discover the ancient heart of Provence where papal history meets legendary winemaking tradition. Your journey begins in Avignon, where centuries of history whisper through golden stone walls, and continues into the sun-drenched vineyards of Châteauneuf du Pape—one of the most revered wine appellations in the world. Your knowledgeable driver will guide you through the terroir, sharing stories of the popes who planted these vines and the winemakers who tend them today. You'll taste wines at two exceptional estates: the welcoming Caves Saint Charles, where you'll explore a curated selection of regional wines in an intimate setting, and the prestigious Domaine de la Barroche, where tradition and elegance pour into every glass. Between tastings, a leisurely lunch at Comptoir de la Mère Germaine will offer a moment to savor the flavors of Provence in a setting that feels like home. This is more than a wine tour—it's an immersion into the soul of southern France.

French electricity runs at 220 volts / 50 cycles. US appliances need an adapter and transformer.

BASIC PHRASES

Bonjour (Hello) · Merci (Thank you) · S'il vous plait (Please) · Pardon (Excuse me). Always greet with Bonjour when entering any shop, restaurant or business.

TIME

France is CET — 6 hours ahead of Eastern Daylight Time (10AM US = 4PM France). The switch between Standard and Daylight Time may not occur on the same date, reducing the difference to 5 hours during that interim period.

USING PHONES IN FRANCE

French numbers have 10 digits starting with 0. Numbers starting with 06/07 are mobile. Country code: +33.
  • From the US: dial 011 + 33 + 9-digit number (drop the leading 0)
  • Within France: dial the full 10-digit number
  • To call the US: dial 001 + area code + number
