Programme Proposé

Option 1: Provence Art & Wine Experience

Our recommended choice for the Hendersons — combining contemporary art, authentic market atmosphere, serious wine tasting at a premier estate, and exceptional dining with panoramic views.

  • 09:00 — Departure from Le Couvent des Minimes in premium Mercedes vehicle with private English-speaking guide
  • 09:45 — Isle-sur-la-Sorgue morning market: authentic Provençal atmosphere, antiques, local produce, artisan stands. Perfect for Sarah's love of local markets and genuine Provence.
  • 11:30 — Private visit to contemporary art foundation or gallery in the Luberon (such as Fondation Vincent Van Gogh Arles or a curated private collection). Tailored to Sarah's contemporary art interests.
  • 13:15 — Lunch at a exceptional restaurant with panoramic views (such as La Bastide de Gordes or similar Michelin-quality establishment). Menu adapted for Sarah (no red meat), local seasonal ingredients, wine pairings available.
  • 15:30 — Private tasting at a prestigious wine estate (serious producer, not tourist cave). Options include Château La Canorgue, Château de Mille, or similar domaine producing exceptional Luberon and Côtes du Rhône wines. Extended tasting with the winemaker or cellar master — ideal for James's serious wine collecting interests.
  • 17:30 — Scenic drive through perched villages: Gordes, Roussillon, or Ménerbes (time permitting, photo stops only)
  • 18:30 — Return to Le Couvent des Minimes

Why This Works for Your Clients

The Hendersons have sophisticated palates from Burgundy and Tuscany — this avoids tourist traps entirely. Morning market immersion gives Sarah the authentic Provence soul she seeks. The art visit is curated, not mainstream. Wine tasting is at producer level with access James will appreciate as a serious collector. Lunch venue delivers the view and quality they expect. Timing respects their 18:30 return request. This is Provence for connoisseurs, not first-timers.

Option 2: Gordes, Isle-sur-la-Sorgue & Châteauneuf-du-Pape

A wine-focused alternative with the legendary Châteauneuf-du-Pape appellation.

  • 09:00 — Departure from Le Couvent des Minimes
  • 09:45 — Gordes village: iconic perched village, narrow streets, panoramic viewpoints
  • 10:45 — Isle-sur-la-Sorgue market experience (as Option 1)
  • 12:30 — Gourmet lunch near Gordes or en route (adapted menu for Sarah)
  • 14:30 — Châteauneuf-du-Pape: private visit and extended tasting at prestigious domaine (Château de Beaucastel, Domaine du Vieux Télégraphe, or similar). World-class wines, vineyard tour, serious cellar experience for James.
  • 16:45 — Return journey through vineyards and Rhône Valley landscapes
  • 18:30 — Arrival Le Couvent des Minimes

Why This Works for Your Clients

Châteauneuf-du-Pape is a pilgrimage for serious collectors like James — legendary terroir, world-renowned estates, wines with gravitas. The morning still delivers Sarah's market fix and Provence beauty. Slightly more wine-weighted than Option 1, but keeps the authentic, non-touristy approach they demand. Best if James prioritizes access to CDP's top producers.

Option 3: Private Luberon Villages & Wine Estates

A villages-and-vineyards day with maximum Luberon immersion.

  • 09:00 — Departure from Le Couvent des Minimes
  • 09:30 — Lourmarin: elegant village, artisan shops, 15th-century château (exterior), café culture
  • 10:45 — Bonnieux or Ménerbes: perched villages, breathtaking viewpoints, authentic Provence atmosphere
  • 12:00 — Wine tasting at first Luberon estate (organic or biodynamic producer preferred)
  • 13:15 — Lunch at countryside restaurant or wine estate (view, local produce, Sarah's dietary needs respected)
  • 15:00 — Second wine estate visit: serious tasting session, cellar tour, direct producer access for James
  • 16:45 — Roussillon ochre cliffs or Lacoste village (quick stop, time permitting)
  • 18:30 — Return to Le Couvent des Minimes

Why This Works for Your Clients

Maximum village immersion without rushing. Two wine estates allow comparison of Luberon styles and terroirs — educational for James. Villages like Lourmarin offer charm without Gordes-level crowds. Still authentic, still gourmet, but more relaxed pacing. Best if they want deeper Luberon exploration rather than single iconic appellation.
